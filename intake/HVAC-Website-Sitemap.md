Overview

Approved sitemap for the residential + commercial HVAC website template.
Mirrors the structural decisions from plumbing and electrical —
two-level depth (every page ≤2 clicks from home), audience-first service
URLs (/residential/\* and /commercial/\*), dedicated service-area pages,
/emergency promoted to top nav, /contact as the single conversion
endpoint (no auto-scheduling) — with an HVAC-specific service taxonomy.

Top-level navigation

**Home** • **Residential** • **Commercial** • **Service Areas** •
**Emergency** • **Promotions** • **About** • **Reviews** • **Blog** •
**Contact**

Counts

Top-level pages: 10

Residential subservice pages: 12

Commercial subservice pages: 10

Service-area pages: 1 per city served (typical metro: 10–25)

Utility / footer pages: 4 (Privacy, Terms, Accessibility, HTML Sitemap)

Total static pages (excluding city pages and blog posts): \~41

What's different vs. plumbing and electrical

Service taxonomy is fully HVAC-specific. AC repair and AC installation
are split into separate pages (same for heating) for stronger SEO on
intent-specific searches. Heat pumps get their own flagship page on the
residential side — the highest-growth HVAC category given federal tax
credits and the electrification push. Tune-ups & maintenance is its own
subservice page in addition to the recurring /maintenance-plans member
program, capturing single-call "AC tune-up \[city\]" searches.
Commercial adds three pages with no plumbing/electrical analog: chiller
services, VRF/VRV systems, and commercial refrigeration.
/maintenance-plans is even more central than in the other trades —
HVAC's recurring twice-yearly tune-up cadence drives the highest member
program participation of any home service.

Navigation Behavior

Header (sticky)

Logo (left) — Primary nav (center/right) — Sticky tap-to-call phone with
24/7 badge (right) — "Request a Quote" secondary button. Mobile:
hamburger reveals nav; phone CTA stays visible at all times.

Mega-menu structure

Residential and Commercial nav items open mega-menus showing their full
subservice list in two columns. Service Areas opens a quick-jump list of
cities. Blog opens recent posts + category links.

Emergency styling

/emergency is styled in alert-red across nav and footer. The page itself
uses a stripped-down layout — single oversized phone CTA, no mega-menu
distractions.

Seasonal CTA rotation

HVAC is uniquely seasonal. Header sub-banner and homepage hero rotate
based on season: spring/summer = AC tune-up + replacement push,
fall/winter = heating tune-up + furnace replacement push. Build this
rotation into the CMS so it's not a manual swap every season.

Footer

Four columns: Company / Residential services / Commercial services /
Quick links. Bottom row: copyright, license \#, accessibility, privacy,
terms, HTML sitemap.

Sticky mobile bar

Persistent bottom bar on mobile: Call | Text | Get a Quote.

No auto-scheduling

All scheduling routes through /contact (form) or phone. No third-party
booking widget — appointments coordinated manually based on technician
availability.

SEO & Crawl Notes

Architecture rules

Flat — every page ≤2 clicks from home. Audience-first URLs
(/residential/\* and /commercial/\*) for keyword separation. Repair and
installation split as separate pages for AC and heating to capture
intent-specific searches.

Schema markup

LocalBusiness + HVACBusiness on every page. Service schema on each
subservice page. FAQPage schema on /faq and any inline FAQ blocks.
AggregateRating + Review on /reviews. Organization on /about.

Internal linking

Every subservice page links to 3–6 related siblings and 1 parent hub.
Every city page links to all subservice pages with city-aware anchor
text. Blog posts link to 1–2 services and 1–2 other posts.

Seasonal content strategy

HVAC search is highly seasonal. Build evergreen pages for every service,
then layer seasonal blog content on top: "AC tune-up before summer in
\[City\]" (March-May), "Furnace inspection checklist" (Sept-Nov), "Heat
pump cold-weather performance" (Dec-Feb). Internal-link the seasonal
posts back to the evergreen service pages.

Flagship keyword targets

Three subservice pages with the strongest commercial intent and rising
search volume:

/residential/heat-pump-installation — federal tax credit (Inflation
Reduction Act), electrification push. Treat as flagship — 1,500+ words,
local cost data, brand variants (Mitsubishi, Daikin, Carrier, Trane),
tax credit explainer.

/residential/ac-installation — high ticket, summer-peaking search. SEER
ratings, brand selection, financing emphasis.

/commercial/preventive-maintenance — highest-value commercial recurring
revenue. Property manager / restaurant operator search intent.

City pages — critical rule

Each /service-areas/{city} page must have unique, locally-relevant copy
(real neighborhoods, response time, local climate context — desert heat,
humid summers, mountain winters, etc.). Templated city pages get
penalized; unique ones rank.
