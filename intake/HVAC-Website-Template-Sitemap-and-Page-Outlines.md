How to Read This Document

This document contains page-by-page outlines for a residential +
commercial HVAC website template, with realistic demo copy you can adapt
per client. Every HVAC client will be different, so the goal is a
strong, opinionated starting point — not finished copy.

Brand voice defaults

  - Confident and direct — homeowners with no AC in 110° heat or no heat
    in a snowstorm want answers fast

  - Comfort-focused — HVAC sells comfort, not just equipment. Frame
    outcomes (cool, warm, quiet, healthy air) not specs

  - Transparent on pricing, brands, and SEER/AFUE — calls out flat-rate
    quotes, multi-brand options, and what the numbers actually mean

  - Modern + electrification-aware — leans into heat pumps, IRA tax
    credits, smart thermostats rather than treating HVAC as an
    old-school trade

SEO conventions

  - Title tag format: "\[Primary Keyword\] in \[City\] | \[Company
    Name\]" — under 60 characters

  - Meta description: 145–160 characters, includes primary keyword + CTA

  - One H1 per page; keyword + city where natural

  - H2/H3 hierarchy used semantically — never skipped for styling

  - Internal links: every page links to 3–6 relevant siblings + 1 parent
    hub

  - Image alt text describes image and includes city/service when
    relevant

  - FAQ schema on FAQ-bearing pages

  - Service schema on each subservice page

Section 3 — When to call us

Most HVAC problems get worse and more expensive the longer they wait.
Call us if:

  - AC is running but not cooling, or short-cycling (turning on and off
    rapidly)

  - Furnace won't ignite, runs cold, or short-cycles

  - Some rooms are way hotter or colder than others

  - Energy bills are climbing with no other explanation

  - Loud, grinding, or screeching noises from indoor or outdoor unit

  - Ice on the outdoor coil or indoor evaporator

  - Water pooling around the indoor unit

  - System is 12+ years old and needs another repair

  - Humidity stays high even with AC running

  - CO detector triggered or you smell gas (also call your gas utility
    immediately)

Section 3 — Why heat pumps now

Three reasons heat pumps moved from niche to mainstream in the last five
years:

  - Cold-climate performance — Modern heat pumps (Mitsubishi Hyper-Heat,
    Carrier Infinity Greenspeed, Lennox SL25XPV) maintain rated capacity
    down to \~5°F and operate effectively below 0°F. The "heat pumps
    don't work in winter" line is decades out of date.

  - Federal incentives — The Inflation Reduction Act 25C tax credit
    covers 30% of equipment + install, up to $2,000 per year, on
    qualifying heat pumps. Stackable with state and utility rebates that
    often add another $500–$2,000.

  - Operating cost — Heat pumps are 2–4x more efficient than electric
    resistance heat and often beat gas furnaces on operating cost in
    \[State\], especially as electricity gets cleaner and gas prices
    rise.

Section 4 — Right-sizing matters more than the brand

The single biggest mistake HVAC contractors make on heat pump installs
is oversizing. An oversized heat pump short-cycles, dehumidifies poorly,
and wears out faster. We do a real Manual J load calculation on every
install — measuring your home's insulation, window U-values,
infiltration, and orientation — to pick a system that actually fits.

Most homes in \[City\] need a 2.5–4 ton heat pump. If a contractor
quotes a 5-ton system without doing a Manual J, walk away.

Section 5 — Our process

5.  Free in-home consultation — We measure your home, inspect the

6.  Manual J load calculation — Real heat-loss and heat-gain math, not a

    the office.

7.  Manual S equipment selection — We pick three equipment options

8.  Detailed flat-rate quote — Equipment, install labor, refrigerant

9.  Installation — Typically a single day for a straightforward swap,

    Comfort-tested before we leave.

10. Tax credit + rebate paperwork — We file your AHRI certificate and
    provide all documentation for the federal 25C credit, state rebates,
